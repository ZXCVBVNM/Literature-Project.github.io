Nathaniel Hawthorne - Works

Works

  • The Scarlet Letter (1850) - A novel set in 17th-century Puritan Massachusetts that explores the consequences of adultery and isolation. The protagonist, Hester Prynne, grapples with societal judgment and personal redemption.
  • The House of the Seven Gables (1851) - A novel centered around a cursed family and a mysterious house with a dark past. Hawthorne weaves a tale of ancestral sins and the possibility of redemption.
  • Twice-Told Tales (1837) - A collection of short stories showcasing Hawthorne's skill in the short story genre. Notable stories include "Young Goodman Brown" and "The Minister's Black Veil."
  • The Marble Faun (1860) - A novel set in Italy, examining the moral and psychological consequences of a murder. The narrative explores the interplay of art, sin, and redemption.
  • The Blithedale Romance (1852) - A novel inspired by Hawthorne's experiences at the Brook Farm utopian community. The story explores the complexities of communal living and the pursuit of an ideal society.
